Your quote & pricing

2.1  Prices include GST. The totals on your quote are the GST-inclusive amounts you pay. A tax invoice showing the GST is provided once your order is confirmed.

2.2  How long a quote lasts. Your quote is valid until the “valid until” date shown on it (typically 30 days from the date we sent it). After that date, prices and availability may change and we may need to re-quote.

2.3  Enquiry vs full quote. An enquiry price guide gives an indication only — it has no quantities and is not a fixed offer. Once we’ve sized your job from measurements, we’ll issue a full quote with quantities and a firm total.

2.4  Measurements & estimates. Where a quote is based on areas or measurements you’ve given us, or that we’ve estimated, quantities are an estimate. Tiles are supplied in whole boxes, so quantities are rounded up. You’re responsible for confirming your own measurements before ordering — see clause 5.

2.5  Genuine errors. Quotes are prepared with care, but if an obvious pricing or product error slips through, we’ll let you know before you commit and either correct it or let you walk away. We won’t hold you to a clear mistake — and we’d ask the same fairness in return.

2.6  What’s included. Your quote covers the goods listed on it. Delivery or freight is only included if it’s shown as a line on the quote. Installation, tiling, plumbing, waterproofing and removal of old materials are not included unless we’ve specifically listed them.

Payment terms

3.1  You can choose one of two ways to pay:

  • Pay in full when you place your order; or
  • Pay a deposit of at least 30% of the order total to confirm and secure your order, with the remaining balance paid later under clause 3.2.

3.2  Balance before goods leave us. If you’ve paid a deposit, the full balance must be paid before your goods are collected, delivered or dispatched — whichever applies to your order. We don’t release, load or hand goods to a carrier until we’ve received payment in full in cleared funds.

3.3  What the deposit does. Your deposit confirms your order, holds your pricing, and lets us order in or set aside your goods. For special-order and indent items, your deposit may be used to place the order with our supplier (see clause 8.3).

3.4  How to pay. We accept the payment methods shown on your invoice (such as EFTPOS, card or bank transfer). Card surcharges, where they apply, are shown at the time of payment. Please quote your job number (e.g. TTH-0000) as the reference on any bank transfer.

3.5  Title. The goods remain our property until you’ve paid for them in full. Until then, you must not on-sell them or treat them as your own, and we may recover them if payment isn’t made.

3.6  If the balance isn’t paid. If your goods are ready and the balance stays unpaid, we’ll hold the order (see storage, clause 6.7). Deposits are dealt with under clause 8.

Tiles, products & quantities

Shade, size & batch variation

5.1  Tiles and natural products are made in batches. Shade, tone, size, texture and finish can vary from batch to batch, and from the sample or display piece you saw in the showroom. This is a normal characteristic of the product, not a fault.

5.2  Order enough in one go. Because of batch variation, please order all you need for the job at once — including extra for cuts, breakages and future repairs. A later top-up order may come from a different batch and may not match. We can’t guarantee a matching batch on re-orders.

Check before you lay

Please have your tiler inspect every box for shade, size, finish and visible faults before any tiles are cut or fixed. Laying a tile is taken as acceptance of it — once tiles are cut or installed we can’t accept a claim for shade variation, sizing or visible faults that should have been seen beforehand.

Natural materials

5.3  Natural stone and other natural products carry variation in colour, veining, marking and pitting as part of their character. Images, samples and displays are a guide only and won’t exactly match what’s supplied.

Quantities are your call

5.4  Where we’ve estimated quantities from measurements, that’s a guide to help you. Final quantities — including allowance for wastage, pattern, layout and cuts (commonly 10% or more) — are your responsibility to confirm. We’re happy to help you work it out, but we can’t be responsible for a shortfall or over-order if the measurements or laying pattern differ from what was quoted.

Samples & displays

5.5  Samples and displays show the general look of a product. The goods supplied will be of similar but not identical appearance, for the reasons in clauses 5.1 and 5.3.

Delivery & collection

Your quote shows which of the three options below applies to your order. The terms for all three are set out here so you can see exactly what to expect.

Collection from our showroom

Option AYour order

6.1  If you’re collecting, here’s how it works:

  • We’ll let you know when your order is ready to collect from Unit 10/1a Wirraway Street, Taminda, during showroom hours (Mon–Fri 8:30am–5pm, Sat 9am–2pm).
  • Please bring your job or quote number, and have the balance paid in full before you collect.
  • Bring a suitable vehicle. Tiles are heavy — a box can weigh 20–30kg and they’re often supplied on a pallet. For large or heavy loads, please bring a hand or two to help. Our team will help load where we safely can.
  • Once goods are loaded onto your vehicle, securing and transporting them safely is your responsibility, and risk in the goods passes to you (clause 6.6).
  • Please check your goods as they’re loaded and raise any shortage or damage before you leave (clause 7).
  • Please collect within 14 days of us letting you know your order is ready — after that, storage may apply (clause 6.7).

Delivery by us (local)

Option BYour order

6.2  Where your quote shows local delivery on our own vehicle:

  • We deliver to the address on your quote for the delivery fee shown. We’ll agree a delivery day with you in advance.
  • We deliver to a single, safe, accessible point at ground level — generally the kerb, driveway or garage. We don’t carry goods inside, upstairs, or onto a work area.
  • Clear, safe access for our vehicle is needed. Please tell us at booking about anything that affects access — narrow driveways, soft ground, low clearance, parking limits.
  • Someone aged 18 or over should be on site to receive and check the delivery. If you ask us to leave goods unattended, that’s at your risk.
  • The balance must be paid in full before we dispatch. Risk passes to you on delivery (clause 6.6).

You’ll need help to unload

Our delivery is often a one-person run, and we can’t safely unload heavy or bulk goods — palletised tiles, baths, vanities and large boxes — on our own. For those orders you must have suitable labour on site (your tiler, builder or a couple of able hands) to help receive and unload. If no one’s available to help and the goods can’t be unloaded safely, we may not be able to complete the delivery, and a re-delivery fee may apply.

Delivery by freight carrier

Option CYour order

6.3  For larger orders or deliveries out of town, we use an independent freight carrier. Where your quote shows freight delivery:

  • The freight charge shown on your quote covers delivery to the address provided. Some areas and larger loads are quoted separately before dispatch.
  • Delivery is made by forklift. You must tell us about any access restrictions at the time of booking.
  • Site access is your responsibility. You need to make sure there’s clear, safe, level access for a forklift and that the address can be reached by a freight vehicle. If access is blocked, unavailable or unsafe when the carrier arrives, the delivery won’t go ahead and a re-delivery fee (charged at the carrier’s standard rate for your zone) may apply.
  • A responsible person aged 18 or over must be on site to receive the goods and check them on arrival.
  • Timing. Lead times are indicative — typically 1–2 business days from the carrier’s pick-up when booked ahead. We can’t give a firm delivery date until the carrier confirms scheduling, so please book early and don’t lock in trades until delivery is confirmed.
  • Once goods are with the carrier, delivery timing is in the carrier’s hands. We’ll help coordinate and follow up, but we can’t guarantee an exact time.
  • The balance must be paid in full before we dispatch to the carrier. Risk passes on dispatch; please see clause 7 for checking goods and reporting transit damage.

Title & risk

6.6  Risk in the goods (responsibility for loss or damage) passes to you when they’re collected by or on behalf of you, or delivered to your address, or handed to a carrier for delivery to you — whichever applies. Ownership stays with us until you’ve paid in full (clause 3.5). We recommend you’re ready to store goods safely and under cover once they’re with you.

If goods aren’t collected or delivery isn’t taken

6.7  If your order is ready and isn’t collected, or a delivery can’t be completed for a reason on your side, within 14 days of us telling you it’s ready, we may charge a fair storage fee while we hold it. We’ll always try to reach you first.

Receiving your goods

7.1  Check on receipt. Please check your goods when you collect or receive them. Count the boxes and look over the items for any obvious damage or shortage.

7.2  Note it then and there. If anything is short or visibly damaged, note it on the delivery docket or carrier’s paperwork before signing, or raise it with our team at collection, and contact us within 48 hours with details and photos. This lets us sort it quickly and, for freight, lodge any claim with the carrier in time.

7.3  Before laying tiles. As set out in clause 5.2, please inspect tiles for shade, size, finish and visible faults before they’re cut or laid. Reporting an issue before installation means we can put it right; once tiles are laid, that option is gone.

7.4  None of this affects your rights for faults that couldn’t reasonably have been seen on a check — see clause 9.

Returns & cancellations

8.1  We want you to be happy with what you buy. Outside your rights under the Australian Consumer Law (clause 9), this is our policy for change-of-mind returns and cancellations.

Stocked items — change of mind

8.2  We’ll accept a return of a regularly-stocked item within 30 days of purchase where it’s unused, in its original undamaged packaging, in resaleable condition, and you have proof of purchase. A restocking fee of up to 20% may apply to cover handling, and any delivery charge isn’t refundable. Refunds are made by the original payment method.

Special-order & indent items

8.3  Special-order, indent and made-to-order items are brought in or made specifically for you and aren’t held as stock. Once that order is placed with our supplier it generally can’t be cancelled, returned or refunded. If you need to cancel, we’ll do what we reasonably can with the supplier, but where they won’t take the goods back your deposit (and, if already supplied, the balance) covers the cost we’ve committed to and may not be refundable. We’ll always be straight with you about what is and isn’t recoverable.

What we can’t take back

8.4  For hygiene, safety and practical reasons we can’t accept change-of-mind returns of: items that have been used, fitted or installed; tiles that have been cut or laid; clearance, discontinued or run-out items sold as such; or goods without their original packaging. This doesn’t apply to faulty goods (clause 9).

Cancelling before goods are ordered

8.5  If you cancel a confirmed order before we’ve ordered or set aside your goods, we’ll refund your deposit less any costs we’ve genuinely incurred to that point.
